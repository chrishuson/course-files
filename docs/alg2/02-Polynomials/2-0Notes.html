Polynomials and Rational Functions

Polynomial (literally, "many numbers", see also polygon and polyhedron)
A polynomial function of xxx is a function given by a sum of terms, each of which is a constant times a whole number power of xxx. The word polynomial is used to refer both to the function and to the expression defining it.
(Illustrative Math lesson 2.1 October 16th)

5x3+2x2+7x+95x^3+2x^2+7x+95x3+2x2+7x+9

The degree of a polynomial is the largest power of xxx that appears in the expression. The leading term of a polynomial is the term with the highest power of xxx. The leading coefficient of a polynomial is the coefficient of the leading term. In the first example above, the degree is 3, the leading term is 5x35x^35x3, and the leading coefficient is 5.

degree
The degree, or the order, of a polynomial in xxx is the highest exponent occuring on xxx when you write the polynomial out as a sum of non-zero constants times powers of xxx (with like terms collected).
(Illustrative Math lesson 2.2 October 17th)

variable
A letter used to represent a value that may change or is unknown, for example xxx or ttt. For a function, the input is the independent variable and the output is the dependent variable.

Example, fff is a second order polynomial function defined as f:y=2x2+5f: y = 2x^2+5f:y=2x2+5, then xxx is the independent variable and yyy is the dependent variable.

coefficient
The value a variable is multiplied by. For example, the term 2x22x^22x2 has a coefficient of 222.

constant
The coefficient for x0x^0x0. The constant is also the yyy-intercept of the function. For example, the polynomial x2+6x+5x^2 + 6x +5x2+6x+5 has a constant of 555, and (0,5)(0,5)(0,5) is the yyy-intercept because f(0)=(0)2+6(0)+5=5f(0) = (0)^2 + 6(0) +5 = 5f(0)=(0)2+6(0)+5=5

solutions, zeros, x-intercepts
The solutions of a polynomial function are the values of xxx for which the function equals zero, f(x)=0f(x)=0f(x)=0. The solutions are also called the zeros of the function, and they are xxx-intercepts of the graph of the function.
(Illustrative Math lesson 2.4 October 23rd)

relative maximum
A point on the graph of a function that is higher than any of the points around it.

relative minimum
A point on the graph of a function that is lower than any of the points around it.
